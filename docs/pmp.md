1	Introduction
Workforce Performance (WFP) is an enterprise-grade, web-based solution developed to transform how organizations measure, manage, and optimize employee performance. The platform’s scope encompasses a comprehensive suite of modules and features tailored to provide a holistic and integrated approach to performance management.
Through our integrated modules, WFP provides a unified platform for managing the entire performance lifecycle, from goal setting and data collection to evaluation, coaching, and rewards. The solution is designed to be highly configurable and scalable, capable of supporting organizations of all sizes and complexities. It integrates seamlessly with existing third-party systems or can be used with manual data uploads through an easy-to-use interface or by uploading Excel files, providing the flexibility needed to adapt to your unique operational environment.

4	Performance Measuring
WFP provides advanced tools to measure the performance of your employees and ensuring that your organization runs smoothly.

4.1	KPIs Builder
The KPIs Builder is the foundational module of the WFP platform, providing a robust and intuitive framework for defining, managing, and leveraging KPIs. In today's data-driven business environment, the ability to accurately measure what matters is paramount.
The KPIs Builder empowers organizations to move beyond generic, one-size-fits-all metrics and craft a performance measurement system that is a true reflection of their unique strategic priorities and operational nuances. This module serves as the central nervous system of the WFP platform, transforming raw data from disparate sources into a coherent, actionable, and intelligent stream of performance insights.
By providing a centralized repository for all performance metrics, the KPIs Builder ensures consistency, transparency, and alignment across the entire organization, from the executive suite to the front-line employees.
WFP supports a variety of data source types, including manual uploads via CSV/Excel files, manual upload through an easy-to-use interface, and automated integration with third-party systems through direct databases integration and through API integrations.
4.2	Performance Sets
Performance Sets are a pivotal innovation that transcends the limitations of generic performance measurement. Performance Sets empower organizations to construct highly customized and contextually relevant performance frameworks, ensuring that every employee is evaluated against metrics that are directly pertinent to their role, department, and skill group.
The core value proposition of Performance Sets lies in their ability to foster fairness, clarity, and precision in performance evaluations. By allowing for differentiated performance standards – for instance, separate KPI targets for an ‘Inbound Agents’ versus an ‘Outbound Agents,’ or varying thresholds for ‘Senior Agents’ compared to ‘Junior Agents’ within the same skill group – WFP ensures that performance expectations are always realistic, equitable, and transparent.
This level of detailed control not only enhances the accuracy of performance assessments but also significantly boosts employee engagement and motivation, as individuals clearly understand how their efforts contribute to broader organizational objectives.
4.3	Scorecards
The Scorecards provide a sophisticated and highly customizable mechanism for structuring and visualizing employee performance evaluations. Moving beyond simple data aggregation, Scorecards transform raw KPI data into meaningful, actionable insights, presenting a holistic view of an individual’s contribution against predefined targets.
At its core, the Scorecards module allows you to design structured evaluation templates that integrate various KPI groups, supporting both percentage based and point-based evaluation systems. This flexibility ensures that organizations can align their performance measurement methodologies with their unique cultural values and operational requirements.
A key advantage of WFP’s Scorecards is their adaptability to the organizational hierarchy. Each scorecard can be meticulously customized and assigned based on role, job level, skill group, ensuring that evaluations are always contextually relevant.
Further customization is achieved through the definition of one or more evaluation groups within each scorecard. Each group is assigned a specific weight, determining its contribution to the overall score, and a ceiling, which sets the maximum achievable score for that group.
Within these groups, administrators define a list of relevant KPIs, each with its own boundary type. The scoring logic for each KPI can be precisely configured, and conditional elements based on job level can be applied, allowing for nuanced adjustments to performance expectations.
Additionally, WFP supports the inclusion of Bonus and Deduction groups, which follow the same structured configuration, enabling organizations to incorporate incentives and penalties directly into the performance evaluation framework. This comprehensive configuration ensures that WFP Scorecards are not just evaluation forms, but dynamic tools that drive performance alignment and accountability.
4.4	Recalculation Tasks
The Recalculation Tasks module is a critical administrative component, designed to ensure the accuracy, integrity, and consistency of KPI data across the entire system. In dynamic business environments, data sources can change, errors can occur, or retroactive adjustments may be necessary. 
This module provides administrators with a powerful and flexible mechanism to manage these scenarios by defining and executing scheduled or on demand tasks that reprocess KPI data for selected users within specified date ranges.
It is the backbone for maintaining data hygiene and ensuring that all performance records reflect the most current and accurate information. 
The value of the Recalculation Tasks module lies in its ability to facilitate seamless data correction and synchronization without manual intervention across individual records. It ensures that any changes to underlying data, KPI definitions, or scoring logic are consistently propagated throughout the system, preventing discrepancies and maintaining the reliability of performance reports. This is particularly crucial for historical data, where accuracy is paramount for trend analysis, auditing, and fair employee evaluations.
The unique advantage of WFP’s Recalculation Tasks is its granular control and flexibility. Administrators can choose from various actions – recalculating scores, fetching updated data from source systems, erasing previously stored data, or applying predefined score adjustments—and apply these actions with precise targeting based on business hierarchy. This comprehensive control over recalculation logic and execution lifecycle ensures that WFP remains a trustworthy and adaptable performance management solution, capable of handling complex data management challenges with efficiency and precision. 
The configuration capabilities of the Recalculation Tasks module are designed to provide administrators with a highly flexible and precise mechanism for defining data reprocessing workflows. This ensures that tasks are executed exactly as needed, targeting specific data sets and applying the correct adjustments.

The module offers three distinct actions for recalculation tasks: 
1. Recalculate
This action reprocesses the KPI scores based on the current definitions and data. It includes an optional feature to re-fetch updated data directly from source systems, ensuring that the latest information is used in the recalculation.

2. Erase Data
This action permanently removes previously stored KPI records for the selected users and date range, useful for cleaning up erroneous data or resetting performance periods.

3. Adjust Value
This action allows administrators to set a custom score value for selected KPIs within the specified range, providing a direct method for applying manual adjustments or corrections.

The operational aspects of the Recalculation Tasks module provide administrators with a clear, centralized view and robust control over all defined data reprocessing workflows. This ensures efficient management of tasks, from monitoring their status to intervening when necessary.
Once created, recalculation tasks appear in a dedicated listing view, providing key details at a glance. This includes the creation date of the task, the specific KPIs affected, the defined date range for the recalculation, and the current status of the task (e.g., ‘Pending,’ ‘In Progress,’ ‘Paused,’ or ‘Done’).
Contextual actions are available for each task, enabling administrators to Pause, Resume, or Delete a task depending on its current status and the operational need. For instance, a task that is ‘In Progress’ might be paused to prioritize another critical operation, or a ‘Pending’ task might be deleted if it is no longer required.

5	Performance Monitoring
The Performance Dashboard module serves as the dynamic, real-time reporting dashboard of the WFP platform. It is meticulously designed to provide employees, team leaders, supervisors, and executives with immediate, interactive insights into KPI progress, coaching sessions, logged events, evaluations, competitions, and rule-based alerts.
The core value of the Performance Dashboard lies in its ability to transform complex data into easily digestible visual representations. The displayed data is dynamically generated based on the KPIs and Performance Sets meticulously configured within the WFP platform, ensuring that every insight is directly relevant to the user's role and responsibilities.
The operational design of the Performance Dashboard emphasizes user centricity and actionable insights, providing several personalized and role-based views categorized under 'My Performance' and 'Team Performance'. While the dashboard does not involve direct user configuration, it dynamically adapts its content and presentation based on the user's assigned roles, teams, and configured performance sets.
Each view is segmented into intuitive tabs (e.g., General, Details, Heat Map), offering diverse visualizations of KPIs through tiles, charts, quartiles, and list views. Users can delve into detailed views, apply filters, interact with alerts, participate in competitions, and even schedule coaching sessions directly from within the dashboard, making it a central hub for all performance-related activities. 
5.1	My Performance 
The 'My Performance' section of the dashboard is specifically tailored for individual employees, providing a comprehensive and engaging overview of their personal performance journey. This view includes a 'Side Card' for quick summaries, a 'General Overview' for a holistic perspective, and 'KPI Details' for granular insights into individual metric performance.
Employees can utilize filters to focus on specific areas of interest, fostering motivation and goal alignment. This personalized space empowers users to actively monitor their progress towards established goals, engage with coaching and event logs, review their evaluations, and even benchmark their performance against peers. The intuitive design and rich data visualization encourage self-management and continuous personal development, making performance tracking an empowering experience rather than a mere reporting exercise. 

5.2	Team Performance 
The 'Team Performance' section is a powerful tool designed for team leaders and supervisors, offering a consolidated and insightful view of their team's collective performance. This module provides grouped employee performance views, enabling managers to quickly assess overall team health and identify trends.

1. Watch List
The Watch List highlights critical KPIs and individuals who need immediate attention, enabling faster, more confident decision-making.
It also identifies outliers and employees whose performance significantly deviates from the norm, whether positively or negatively. By surfacing these exceptions, managers can quickly recognize top performers who deserve acknowledgment and address struggling employees who may require additional support, coaching, or resources. This proactive visibility helps prevent small issues from escalating while ensuring high achievers remain engaged and motivated.
Quartiles View
The Quartiles View provides a clear picture of performance distribution across the team, spotlighting both top and bottom performers to guide targeted actions.
Breakdown View
The Breakdown View presents performance data in detail, with the flexibility to switch between different time intervals – such as 15 minutes, 30 minutes, hourly, daily, weekly, or monthly. This adaptability allows managers to zoom in for real-time insights or step back to identify broader trends, ensuring that both short-term actions and long-term strategies are guided by accurate, timely information.
Heat Map
The Heat Map transforms performance data into an intuitive visual grid, allowing managers to spot top and bottom performers at a glance. With the ability to zoom in for deeper detail, it provides a fast and effective way to uncover patterns, trends, and areas that need immediate attention.
5.3	Rules Builder
The Rules Builder module is a powerful automation engine that enables organizations to define, implement, and manage sophisticated rule-based alerts, notifications, and automated actions. In a complex operational environment, manual monitoring and intervention can be time consuming, prone to error, and reactive. The Rules Builder transforms performance management into a proactive discipline, allowing organizations to automate responses to specific performance triggers, ensuring timely interventions and consistent adherence to operational protocols.
The value of the Rules Builder lies in its ability to create an intelligent, self-regulating performance ecosystem. By establishing predefined conditions and corresponding actions, organizations can automatically identify deviations from performance targets, trigger alerts for managers, notify employees of critical thresholds, or even initiate automated processes such as coaching session assignments.
This automation significantly reduces administrative overhead, minimizes response times to critical events, and ensures that performance management policies are applied consistently and without bias.
This interconnectedness allows rules to be built upon any KPI, creating a highly responsive and adaptive system. Whether it’s flagging an agent whose KPI falls below a certain threshold, sending an automated congratulatory message for exceeding a target, or scheduling a follow-up action based on an evaluation outcome, the Rules Builder empowers organizations to operationalize their performance strategies with unprecedented efficiency and precision.
The configuration capabilities of the Rules Builder module provide administrators with an intuitive yet powerful interface to define complex logical conditions and their corresponding automated actions. This ensures that rules are precisely tailored to meet specific operational needs and performance management objectives.
5.4	Disputes
The Disputes module is a critical component designed to uphold transparency, fairness, and accountability in performance management. It provides a structured and auditable mechanism for employees to formally challenge KPI results or any performance data they believe to be inaccurate or unfairly represented.
In any robust performance management system, the ability for employees to voice concerns and seek clarification is paramount to maintaining trust and ensuring the integrity of the data. This module empowers employees with a clear process for raising such challenges, while simultaneously equipping managers and KPI owners with efficient workflows to review, approve, or reject these disputes. 
The value of the Disputes module extends beyond mere conflict resolution; it fosters a culture of open communication and data accuracy. By providing a formal channel for feedback, it helps identify potential data discrepancies, system errors, or misunderstandings in performance calculations, leading to continuous improvement of the entire WFP system.
The operational framework of the Disputes module is meticulously designed to facilitate a clear, efficient, and transparent resolution process, with distinct listing pages and functionalities tailored to each role involved in the dispute lifecycle. This role-based access ensures that users interact with the system in a manner consistent with their responsibilities, streamlining workflows and enhancing accountability. 
The disputes module separates the views per role.

1. My Disputes (for Employees)
This view provides employees with a comprehensive overview of all disputes they have submitted. From this interface, employees can initiate new disputes, track the real-time status of their pending challenges (e.g., ‘Submitted,’ ‘Under Review,’ ‘Approved,’ ‘Rejected’), and view the details of each dispute, including any manager or KPI owner comments. This empowers employees to stay informed and engaged throughout the resolution process.

2. Pending Disputes (for Managers)
Team leaders and supervisors access this view to manage disputes submitted by their direct reports. This centralized dashboard allows managers to review pending disputes, assess the validity of the employee’s claim, and take appropriate action – either approving the dispute or rejecting it with a clear explanation. The interface provides necessary context and tools to make informed decisions efficiently.

3. My KPIs Disputes (for KPI Owners)
This view is designed for individuals or departments responsible for the integrity and accuracy of specific KPIs. KPI owners have the ultimate authority in the dispute resolution process. From this interface, they can review disputes related to their owned KPIs, validate data, and make final decisions. This includes the ability to modify scores directly within the system if a dispute is approved, or to take bulk actions across similar disputes if a systemic issue is identified. This ensures that data corrections are applied consistently and accurately.
